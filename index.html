Invisible Women

By Carolina Criado Perez

The book

The first thing I would like to point out is that this is THE BOOK for anyone interested in gender equality.

This book allows us to understand how systemic the gender gap is. The author provides data and scientific studies to proove this point, also making us realize that even if we know about gender inequlities, the degree is so deep, that we cannot even imagine.

In my case, it is my Bible. I have read it, and studied it several times

Invisible Women: Exposing data Bias in a World Designeg for Men was published in March 2019 by Chatto & Windus in the UK & Abrams in the US. It is being translated into 30 languages, and is the winner of the 2019 Royal Society Science Book Prize, the 2019 Books Are My Bag Readers Choice Award, and the 2019 Financial Times Business Book of the Year Award.

The author

Caroline Criado Perez is a journalist, feminist, writer, speaker, and activist from England.

Caroline was the 2013 recipient of the Liberty Human Rights Campaigner of the Year award, and was named OBE in the Queen’s Birthday Honours 2015. In 2020 she was the recipient of Finland’s HÄN award for promoting equality, and in 2021 she received an honourary doctorate from the University of Lincoln.

She writes a weekly newsletter keeping up with the latest on the gender data gap at newsletter.carolinecriadoperez.com
